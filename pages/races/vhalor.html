Vhalor

The Vhalor carry the blood of ancient dragons through their veins, and every aspect of their being reflects this draconic heritage. Tall and imposing, with scales that catch the light and eyes that smolder with inner fire, they command attention and respect in equal measure. Their ancestry traces back to the great wyrms of the Age of Flame, when the world was still young and dragons ruled the skies.

Despite the fearsome reputation that precedes them, the Vhalor are a deeply honorable people. Their society is built on ancient codes of conduct, oaths, and the pursuit of personal excellence. To break one's word as a Vhalor is considered the greatest of shames.

Racial Traits

Draconic Ancestry

You have draconic ancestry. Choose one dragon type from the Draconic Ancestry table. Your breath weapon and damage resistance are determined by the dragon type, as shown in the table.

Breath Weapon

You can use your action to exhale destructive energy. Your draconic ancestry determines the size, shape, and damage type of the exhalation. When you use your breath weapon, each creature in the area of the exhalation must make a saving throw. The DC for this saving throw equals 8 + your Constitution modifier + your proficiency bonus. A creature takes 2d6 damage on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. The damage increases to 3d6 at 6th level, 4d6 at 11th level, and 5d6 at 16th level. After you use your breath weapon, you can't use it again until you complete a short or long rest.

Damage Resistance

You have resistance to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry.

Darkvision

You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Draconic Presence

You have proficiency in the Intimidation or Persuasion skill (your choice). Your draconic heritage radiates power that others feel instinctively.

Languages

You can speak, read, and write Common and Draconic. Draconic is thought to be one of the oldest languages and is often used in the study of magic.

Subraces

The Vhalor are divided into four distinct lineages, each tied to a different draconic form. Your subrace determines additional traits and refines your draconic heritage.

Brizaer
Western Dragon

The Brizaer descend from the great western dragons, bearing broad wings, powerful builds, and an affinity for elemental breath. They are the most commonly encountered Vhalor and carry the classical dragon silhouette.

STR +1 Winged (limited)
Manatage
Eastern Dragon

The Manatage carry the blood of eastern dragons, with long serpentine features, iridescent scales, and a deep connection to fortune and weather. They are considered harbingers of change.

Descending from the powerful flightless drakes, the Vuxnes trade aerial ability for incredible ground-based power. They are built lower and broader, with exceptional physical endurance and pack hunter instincts.

Society & Lore

The Age of Flame

Vhalor historians trace their origins to the great dragons of the Age of Flame, when draconic beings walked the world as gods. The Vhalor are believed to be the descendants of these ancient beings who chose to walk among the younger races, giving up full draconic form for something more grounded.

Honor Codes

Vhalor society is governed by an ancient system of honor called the Vharak. Every Vhalor swears oaths of conduct from a young age, and breaking these oaths carries severe social and sometimes physical consequences. An oath-breaker is called a Vhask — a word with no positive connotation.
