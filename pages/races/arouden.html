Arouden

The Arouden are a proud and resilient bovine people, their bodies built for endurance and their spirits tempered by the open plains and high peaks. They possess broad, powerful frames, crowned with impressive horns that vary in shape and size among their many lineages. Their eyes hold the deep calm of grazing herds but can flash with terrifying fury when their kin are threatened.

Arouden society is communal and deeply rooted in tradition. They honor ancestors, protect the herd above all, and value strength paired with wisdom. To an Arouden, a promise is sacred, and hospitality is an unbreakable law. Their smiths are renowned for crafting durable armor from mountain ores, and their warriors form shield-walls that have never been broken.

Racial Traits

Horns

Your horns are natural melee weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with them, you deal piercing damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier, instead of the bludgeoning damage normal for an unarmed strike.

Powerful Build

You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

Stampeding Charge

If you move at least 20 feet straight toward a target and then hit it with a Horns attack on the same turn, the target takes an extra 2d6 damage. If the target is a creature, it must succeed on a Strength saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier) or be pushed up to 10 feet away and knocked prone.

Herd Resilience

You have advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed. When you can see an allied creature within 30 feet, you also have advantage on Constitution saving throws.

Darkvision

You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Languages

You can speak, read, and write Common and Arouden (a guttural, low-tongue filled with deep vowels).

Society & Lore

The Great Herds

Arouden nomadic tribes, known as "Herds," follow ancestral migration routes that have existed for millennia. These paths are considered sacred, and to block them is an act of war. Each Herd is led by a council of elders and a War-Chieftain chosen by ritual combat.

The Horned Pact

Arouden believe that their horns store the memories of their ancestors. Before battle, they ritually etch their horns with oaths. Breaking an oath is to "shatter one's horns," a fate worse than death that leads to exile.
