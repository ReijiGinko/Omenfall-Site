Belnorn

Massive and imposing, the Belnorn carry the strength and calm of bears within their blood. Thick fur, powerful limbs, and a low, rumbling voice that can shake the timbers of a hall—they are creatures of deep forests and high mountains. Despite their fearsome appearance, they are often slow to anger, preferring contemplation to combat, but their wrath once roused is legendary.

Belnorn live in secluded clans that revere the old spirits of nature. They are master carpenters, healers, and keepers of druidic lore. Their claws are used as tools as often as weapons, and they have an uncanny ability to find safe shelter and food even in the harshest winters.

Racial Traits

Claws

Your claws are natural weapons, which you can use to make unarmed strikes. If you hit with them, you deal slashing damage equal to 1d6 + your Strength modifier.

Thick Hide

When you aren't wearing armor, your AC equals 13 + your Constitution modifier. You can use a shield and still gain this benefit.

Hibernate Trance

You can enter a trance-like state instead of sleeping. After a long rest you gain temporary hit points equal to your level.

Keen Smell

You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on smell.

Darkvision

You can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light.

Society & Lore

Clan of the Sleeping Mountain

Belnorn clans live in harmony with nature, often hibernating together in massive winter dens. They are legendary storytellers.

The First Paw Oath

Belnorn never strike first in anger, but once an enemy is marked, they pursue it to the ends of the earth.
